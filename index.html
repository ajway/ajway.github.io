WHO WE ARE
Alpha Kappa Lambda (ΑΚΛ) is an American collegiate social fraternity for men founded at the University of California, Berkeley, in 1914. In response to the suggestion of Benjamin Ide Wheeler, president of the University of California, we organized our house club as a national fraternity.Through Alpha Kappa Lambda, men with different backgrounds but similar ideals unite with a common purpose: to foster excellence in scholarship, leadership, individual growth, and involvement in community service.

WHAT WE DO
Philanthropy
Alpha Kappa Lambda realizes that sexual assault is a serious problem on college campuses and believes that it can be fought through education, service, and philanthropic projects. Every year, our national philanthropy, These Hands Don’t Hurt, raises awareness for domestic violence and sexual assault prevention.
Alumni Connections
Alpha Kappa Lambda’s distinguished alumni from UC Berkeley retain their bond to the fraternity and often reunite at the house every year. Their experiences and wisdom are passed onto our current brothers and give us opportunities for networking in various fields of work.
Community Service
Every year, Alpha Kappa Lambda gives back to the community through the program Berkeley Project. Our brothers work together with members of the community to do everything from building playgrounds and planting trees, to making meals for the homeless and helping out at local senior centers.
